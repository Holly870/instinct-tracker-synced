What Instinct Is

Each character has an Instinct Score from 0 to 5. It represents how deeply their animal nature has surfaced — 0 is full human control, 5 is the body operating at peak biological efficiency.

Every level unlocks a passive ability that is always active, an active ability always available at that level, and a penalty tied to the creature's specific biology. All passives from lower levels are retained as the score rises. Only the current level's active applies.

Raising the Score

The DM delivers instinct suggestions during play — sensory cues, bodily pulls, the creature's biological logic intruding on the player's mind. Acting on a suggestion raises the score by 1.

Players may also raise their own score by voluntarily choosing the animal's logic over the tactically smarter human choice, unprompted. The DM decides if the moment qualifies.

Resisting & Lowering

A player may resist any instinct suggestion. Resisting requires a Wisdom saving throw: DC 10 + current instinct level.

To actively lower their score, a player must successfully resist a number of suggestions equal to their current level. Level 1 requires 1 resist. Level 4 requires 4. Each success counts toward the total until the score drops by 1, then the counter resets.

DC & Resistance Reference
Level 0 — no saves needed
Level 1 — DC 11  ⋅  resist 1x to lower
Level 2 — DC 12  ⋅  resist 2x to lower
Level 3 — DC 13  ⋅  resist 3x to lower
Level 4 — DC 14  ⋅  resist 4x to lower
Level 5 — DC 15  ⋅  resist 5x to lower

Design note — The DM is the primary voice of each creature's instincts. Suggestions come from the body speaking to the mind. Player-triggered raises represent the mind choosing to listen before being asked.
